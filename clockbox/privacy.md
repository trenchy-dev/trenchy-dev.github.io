# Privacy Policy for Clockbox

**Last updated: May 25, 2026**

This Privacy Policy describes how Clockbox ("the App", "we", "us") handles your information. Clockbox is published by Lyght.

We've kept this short and plain-English because the answer is short: **Clockbox does not collect, transmit, or share any of your data.**

## 1. Data we collect

**We collect nothing.** Clockbox does not send your data to us, our servers, or any third party.

## 2. Data stored on your device

Clockbox stores the following on your iPhone and iPad, and (if you enable iCloud sync) in your private iCloud account:

- **Shift records.** Clock-in and clock-out times, breaks, manual edits, and the source of each event (manual, NFC, Siri, geofence).
- **Workplace settings.** If you set up a workplace for geofence-based auto-clocking: the workplace name, location coordinates, and geofence radius you chose.
- **App preferences.** UI selections such as your last-used calendar view and similar non-personal app state.

This data lives on your device(s). Lyght never receives a copy.

## 3. iCloud sync

If you are signed in to iCloud and have iCloud Drive enabled, Clockbox uses Apple's CloudKit to sync the data in section 2 between your own devices. This sync uses your **private** iCloud database, which is visible only to you. Apple's own [iCloud Privacy Policy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/) governs this storage.

Lyght has no ability to read, access, or recover your iCloud data.

## 4. Location

Clockbox uses your device's location **only for the optional geofence feature** that auto-detects when you arrive at or leave a workplace you have configured. When this feature is enabled:

- Location is processed entirely **on your device** by Apple's CoreLocation framework.
- Location is **never transmitted** off your device — not to us, not to any third party.
- The "Always" location permission is requested only when you save your first workplace; this is required for region monitoring to work while the app is in the background. You can revoke it at any time in Settings → Clockbox → Location.

If you don't set up a workplace, Clockbox does not request location at all.

## 5. Third-party services

Clockbox does not use any third-party analytics, advertising, attribution, crash reporting, or tracking SDKs. The app contains no third-party network code.

The only third-party software embedded in the app is [Lottie](https://github.com/airbnb/lottie-ios), a rendering library for animations — Lottie does not make any network connections.

## 6. Analytics, tracking, advertising

None. Clockbox does not include analytics, behavioral tracking, advertising identifiers (IDFA), fingerprinting, or any cross-app tracking.

## 7. Children's privacy

Clockbox is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone (see section 1).

## 8. Your rights — deleting your data

Because your data lives on your device and in your private iCloud account, you control it directly:

- **On-device data:** delete the app from your iPhone or iPad. iOS removes Clockbox's local data automatically.
- **iCloud data:** Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Account Storage → Clockbox → Delete Data.

There is no developer account or server to contact for deletion because we never received your data in the first place.

## 9. Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top and post the new version at the same URL. If a change is material, we'll also mention it in an in-app release note.
